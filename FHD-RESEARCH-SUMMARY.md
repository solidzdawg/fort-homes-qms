## KEY DISCOVERIES FROM RESEARCH

### 1. Regulatory Landscape

**HUD Code is Comprehensive & Clear**
- All field installation work must comply with 24 CFR 3280 (Manufacturing Standards)
- On-site completion procedures defined in 24 CFR 3282.601-609 (Subpart M)
- Manufacturer retains responsibility for all on-site work quality
- IPIA (Independent Production Inspection Agency) required for final approval

**Colorado Context Favorable**
- State allows both HUD-certified modular AND IRC-compliant modular structures
- Colorado actively encourages modular construction for efficiency
- Clear regulatory path established by state authorities

### 2. FHD Field Operations Structure

**7 Installation Phases Identified & Mapped:**
```
FI-001: Site Preparation & Foundation (3-7 days)
FI-002: Module Transport & Delivery (1-2 days)
FI-003: Module Placement & Leveling (1-2 days)
FI-004: Module Connection & Sealing (2-3 days)
FI-005: Utility Final Connections (3-5 days)  ‚Üê Most Complex
FI-006: Site Finishing (2-5 days)
FI-007: Final Inspection & Customer Handoff (1 day)

Total Installation Timeline: 12-14 days target
```

**Critical Holdpoints Identified:**
- FI-001: Foundation certification, utility clearance
- FI-003: Leveling verification, pier inspection
- FI-005: Licensed trade certifications (electrician, plumber, gas technician)
- FI-007: IPIA final on-site inspection (mandatory before occupancy)

## STRATEGIC DECISIONS MADE

### ‚úÖ HYBRID APPROACH CONFIRMED (Option C)
**Decision:** Maintain separate repositories with clear integration points
- **Factory Repository:** `fort-homes-qms` - Unchanged (28 SOPs + 8 phases)
- **Field Repository:** `fort-homes-development-qms` (new) - 7 installation phases + 8 field QMS procedures
- **Shared Core:** Adaptation of 5-6 SOPs (document control, training, CAPA, etc.)

**Why This Works:**
- Maintains factory accountability for module quality
- Gives FHD autonomy for field operations
- Enables multi-site FHD expansion
- Simplifies regulatory compliance (separate IPIA oversight if needed)
- Allows independent evolution of procedures based on operational experience

### ‚úÖ COMPREHENSIVE HOLDPOINT STRATEGY
**Decision:** Implement 3-tier holdpoint approach
1. **Critical Holdpoints** ‚Üí IPIA inspection required (e.g., leveling, electrical testing, final inspection)
2. **Inspection Holdpoints** ‚Üí FHD QA inspector required (e.g., sealing, connections, torque verification)
3. **Supervisory Holdpoints** ‚Üí Field supervisor sign-off only (e.g., cleanup, material ordering)

**Benefit:** Balances rigorous quality with operational efficiency

### ‚úÖ LICENSED TRADE INTEGRATION
**Decision:** FHD coordinates with, but doesn't replace, licensed professionals
- Electricians for final electrical connections
- Plumbers for water/sewer connections
- Gas technicians for gas connections
- FHD verifies certifications and coordinates schedules

**Regulatory Requirement:** HUD Code requires licensed work per local codes

### ‚úÖ TECHNOLOGY-FIRST APPROACH
**Decision:** Implement mobile-first QMS platform from Day 1
- Field teams work on tablets/phones, not paper
- Real-time photo documentation with timestamps
- Digital holdpoint sign-offs
- Automatic IPIA notification for inspection scheduling
- Customer progress portal

**Competitive Advantage:** First-mover advantage in digital field operations
