## üìê STEP-BY-STEP ASSEMBLY

### STEP 1: Position & Level Chassis (30 min)

**DO THIS:**
1. Forklift operator lifts chassis into bay center
2. Lower onto shims/blocks
3. Check level BOTH directions with 6 ft level
4. Adjust shims until bubble centered (¬±¬º")
5. Measure diagonals corner-to-corner

**CRITICAL MEASUREMENTS:**
- Diagonal A: ________ inches
- Diagonal B: ________ inches
- **Difference MUST be ‚â§¬Ω"** - If not, STOP and adjust chassis position

**‚úÖ PASS CRITERIA:**

**‚ùå REJECT IF:**

### STEP 2: Install Rim Boards (45 min)

**DO THIS:**
1. Measure chassis sides, cut rim boards to length
2. Apply ¬º" bead of adhesive on chassis top flange
3. Set rim board onto adhesive, press firmly
4. Nail with 16d nails @ **12" on center**
5. Check for adhesive squeeze-out (should see it along 80% of board)

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 3:** [Adhesive bead applied to chassis - show proper ¬º" bead size]

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 4:** [Rim board installed with visible adhesive squeeze-out]

**COMMON MISTAKES:**
- ‚ùå No adhesive squeeze-out = not enough adhesive or not pressed down
- ‚ùå Gaps at corners >‚Öõ" = mismeasured or bad cut
- ‚ùå Nails not at 12" spacing = will fail inspection

**‚úÖ PASS:** Adhesive visible, corners tight, nails at 12" O.C.

### STEP 3: Layout & Install Joists (3 hours)

**DO THIS:**
1. Mark joist locations on rim boards: **16" on center**
2. Start from one end, mark every 16" with square
3. Number each location (1, 2, 3...)
4. Apply ¬º" adhesive bead where joist will sit
5. Set I-joist on marks, press into adhesive
6. Nail through rim board into joist (3 nails minimum per end)
7. Install blocking at mid-span (if required by plan)

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 5:** [Layout marks on rim board showing 16" spacing with numbers]

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 6:** [I-joist positioned on adhesive, showing crown up orientation]

**CRITICAL:** Joist crown MUST face UP - sight down joist to find crown

**CHECK SPACING:** Measure every 3rd joist
- Joist 1-2: ________ (target: 16" ¬±¬º")
- Joist 5-6: ________ (target: 16" ¬±¬º")
- Joist 10-11: ________ (target: 16" ¬±¬º")

**COMMON MISTAKES:**
- ‚ùå Spacing drifts - measure from starting point, not last joist
- ‚ùå Joists twist - need blocking or temporary bracing
- ‚ùå Crown down - floor will sag

### STEP 4: Snap Chalk Lines for Fasteners (10 min)

**DO THIS:**
1. Snap chalk lines across floor at **16" intervals** (over joists)
2. First line should be at first joist centerline
3. Verify lines align with joist centers below

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 7:** [Chalk lines snapped on subfloor showing 16" spacing]

**WHY:** Lines guide nailing so you HIT joists (no "shiners")

### STEP 5: Install Subfloor Sheathing (4 hours)

**DO THIS:**
1. Apply ¬º" adhesive bead on TOP of each joist
2. Position first panel at corner, perpendicular to joists
3. Leave **‚Öõ" gap at perimeter** (expansion space)
4. Walk on panel to press adhesive before nailing
5. Nail along chalk lines:
   - **Edges (perimeter): 6" on center**
   - **Field (interior joists): 12" on center**
6. Install next panel with ‚Öõ" gap, stagger end joints by 4 ft
7. Check for adhesive squeeze-out between panel and joist

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 8:** [Adhesive bead on joist - show proper ¬º" bead]

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 9:** [First panel positioned showing ‚Öõ" gap at edge with measurement]

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 10:** [Nailing pattern - show 6" spacing at edge vs 12" in field]

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 11:** [Adhesive squeeze-out visible between panel and joist]

**FASTENER PATTERN (MEMORIZE THIS):**
```
Edge of panel (on rim board):  Nail every 6"
Interior joists:                Nail every 12"
Nail ON the chalk line:         Hits joist center
```

**COMMON MISTAKES:**
- ‚ùå "Shiners" (nails miss joist) - nail on chalk line!
- ‚ùå Nails overdriven (dimple in sheathing) - reduce air pressure to 100 psi
- ‚ùå No expansion gap - floor will buckle when wood expands
- ‚ùå End joints align - creates weak point, always stagger by 4 ft

**CHECK AS YOU GO:**
- Walk on installed panels - should be solid, no squeaks or movement
- Tap nails with hammer - should be flush and tight
- Look for adhesive squeeze-out - should see it at 80% of joist contacts

### STEP 6: Mark Wall & MEP Layout (30 min)

4. Mark MEP penetration locations with paint/marker:

   - Label "P1, P2" for plumbing
   - Label "H1, H2" for HVAC
   - Include size: "P1 - 3" DWV"

**üñºÔ∏è PHOTO 13:** [MEP penetration marked with label and size]
