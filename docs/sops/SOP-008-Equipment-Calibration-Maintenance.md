## 1. Purpose

To establish procedures for identifying measurement and test equipment, calibrating devices to ensure accuracy, performing preventive and corrective maintenance, and maintaining records to verify all production and inspection equipment functions correctly.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to:
- Measurement tools (tape measures, calipers, levels)
- Fastening equipment (nail guns, screw guns)
- Testing equipment (moisture meters, structural testers)
- Production equipment (saws, drill presses, framing tools)
- Inspection equipment (gauge blocks, inspection fixtures)

---

## 3. References & Standards

- ANSI/NIST Standards (calibration)
- SOP-001 (Document Control)

## 4. Equipment Identification & Register

### 4.1 Equipment List

Maintain master list of all equipment:
- Equipment ID number
- Description and model
- Manufacturer specs/tolerances
- Calibration interval
- Maintenance schedule
- Location/owner
- Acquisition date

---

## 5. Calibration Program

### 5.1 Calibration Intervals

| Equipment Type | Interval | Standard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Measurement tools | Annually | NIST-traceable labs |
| Electrical testers | Annually | ANSI |
| Inspection fixtures | Semi-annually | Internal |
| Production equipment | Per manufacturer | Manufacturer |

### 5.2 Calibration Procedure

1. **Schedule calibration** per interval
2. **Send to certified lab** (external calibration)
3. **Receive calibration certificate**
4. **Document results** (passed/failed)
5. **Apply calibration stickers** with due date
6. **Return to service** or repair if failed

### 5.3 Out-of-Calibration Equipment

- Immediately remove from service
- Apply "OUT OF CALIBRATION" tag
- Notify users
- Repair and recalibrate if possible
- Replace if unable to calibrate

---

## 6. Preventive Maintenance

### 6.1 Maintenance Schedule

| Equipment | Action | Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Power tools | Battery charge, safety check | Daily |
| Fastening equipment | Cleaning, jam clearing | Weekly |
| Major equipment | Inspection, lubrication | Monthly |

### 6.2 Maintenance Records

Document:
- Date performed
- Action taken
- Technician name
- Issues found/corrected
- Parts replaced
- Next scheduled maintenance

---

## 7. Corrective Maintenance

When equipment fails:
1. Remove from service immediately
2. Tag "OUT OF SERVICE"
3. Repair by authorized technician
4. Test and verify functionality
5. Recalibrate if needed
6. Return to service with sign-off

---

## 8. Records & Documentation

Maintain:
- Equipment register
- Calibration certificates
- Maintenance logs
- Repair records
- Out-of-service notifications
