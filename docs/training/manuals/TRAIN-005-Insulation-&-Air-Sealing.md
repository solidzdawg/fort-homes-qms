## ðŸŽ¯ TRAINING OVERVIEW

### Purpose
This training manual provides comprehensive instruction for all personnel involved in Phase 5 production operations. The training program is designed to ensure:

- âœ… **Safety**: All workers understand and follow safety requirements
- âœ… **Quality**: Production meets HUD Code and company standards
- âœ… **Efficiency**: Workers can perform tasks to productivity targets
- âœ… **Compliance**: All regulatory and certification requirements are met

### Training Philosophy

Fort Homes follows the **"See One, Do One, Teach One"** methodology:

1. **See One**: Observation and lecture-based learning
2. **Do One**: Hands-on practice under supervision
3. **Teach One**: Demonstrate mastery by training others

This approach, combined with **NREL workforce training best practices**, ensures competency through:
- Hands-on training at actual workstations
- Independent verification at each step
- Quality assurance checkpoints
- Continuous improvement feedback

## ðŸ“š TRAINING MODULES


### Module 1: Phase 5 Safety and Personal Protective Equipment

**Module ID**: TM-501
**Duration**: 2 hours
**Level**: Beginner
**Prerequisites**: None

#### Learning Objectives

- Identify all safety hazards in this phase
- Demonstrate proper PPE usage
- Explain emergency procedures
- Recognize and report unsafe conditions

#### Module Content


**1. Safety Hazard Identification** (LECTURE) - 30 min

**Materials**:
- Safety presentation
- Hazard photos
- OSHA guidelines

**Instructions**:
Review all potential hazards including electrical, fall, pinch points, and material handling risks.




**2. PPE Donning and Doffing** (DEMONSTRATION) - 30 min

**Materials**:
- Safety glasses
- Hard hat
- Gloves
- Steel-toe boots

**Instructions**:
Demonstrate correct procedures for putting on and removing PPE.


**âš ï¸ Safety Notes**:
- Inspect PPE before each use
- Report damaged equipment immediately



**3. Safety Equipment Practice** (HANDS-ON) - 45 min

**Materials**:
- Fire extinguisher trainer
- First aid kit
- Emergency stop buttons

**Instructions**:
Practice using emergency equipment under supervision.




**4. Safety Knowledge Check** (QUIZ) - 15 min

**Materials**:
- Quiz form
- Pencil

**Instructions**:
Complete 20-question safety assessment.




#### Assessment

**Type**: both
**Passing Score**: 80%


**Question 1** (5 points):
What PPE is required for this phase?

**Answer**: All of the above: safety glasses, hard hat, gloves, steel-toe boots



**Question 2** (10 points):
Demonstrate proper use of safety equipment


**Rubric**: Student correctly identifies, inspects, and uses safety equipment

### Module 2: Phase 5 Tools and Equipment Operation

**Module ID**: TM-502
**Duration**: 4 hours
**Level**: Intermediate
**Prerequisites**: TM-501

#### Learning Objectives

- Identify all tools and equipment for this phase
- Demonstrate safe operation of each tool
- Perform basic maintenance and inspection
- Troubleshoot common issues

#### Module Content


**1. Tool Identification and Specifications** (LECTURE) - 45 min

**Materials**:
- Tool catalog
- Specification sheets
- Calibration records

**Instructions**:
Review each tool, its purpose, specifications, and calibration requirements.




**2. Safe Operation Procedures** (DEMONSTRATION) - 1 hour

**Materials**:
- Actual tools
- Safety guards
- Lockout/tagout equipment

**Instructions**:
Demonstrate correct operation, including startup, use, and shutdown procedures.




**3. Supervised Tool Practice** (HANDS-ON) - 2 hours

**Materials**:
- Practice materials
- Tools
- Work instructions

**Instructions**:
Students practice using each tool under direct supervision.


**âš ï¸ Safety Notes**:
- One student per tool
- Instructor must approve each operation
- Emergency stop within reach



**4. Competency Demonstration** (PRACTICAL) - 15 min per student

**Materials**:
- Competency checklist
- Actual work materials

**Instructions**:
Each student demonstrates proficiency with all required tools.




#### Assessment

**Type**: practical
**Passing Score**: 90%


**Question 1** (20 points):
Set up and safely operate pneumatic nail gun


**Rubric**: Student demonstrates: proper PPE, tool inspection, correct pressure setting, safe handling, accurate placement, proper shutdown


**Question 2** (10 points):
Identify when tool requires calibration


**Rubric**: Student correctly checks calibration sticker, identifies out-of-spec conditions

### Module 3: Phase 5 Quality Standards and Inspection Procedures

**Module ID**: TM-503
**Duration**: 3 hours
**Level**: Intermediate
**Prerequisites**: TM-501, TM-502

#### Learning Objectives

- Explain all quality requirements for this phase
- Perform self-inspection using checklists
- Identify defects and nonconformances
- Complete inspection documentation

#### Module Content


**1. HUD Code and Quality Standards** (LECTURE) - 1 hour

**Materials**:
- HUD Code excerpts
- Quality manual
- Specification sheets

**Instructions**:
Review all applicable codes, standards, and company quality requirements.




**2. Inspection Techniques** (DEMONSTRATION) - 45 min

**Materials**:
- Measuring tools
- Inspection forms
- Good/bad examples

**Instructions**:
Demonstrate proper measurement techniques, visual inspection, and documentation.




**3. Practice Inspections** (HANDS-ON) - 1 hour

**Materials**:
- Sample assemblies
- Inspection tools
- Checklists

**Instructions**:
Students perform inspections on prepared samples with known defects.




**4. Quality Knowledge Assessment** (QUIZ) - 15 min

**Materials**:
- Assessment form
- Reference materials

**Instructions**:
Complete quality standards assessment.




#### Assessment

**Type**: both
**Passing Score**: 85%


**Question 1** (5 points):
What is the tolerance for floor levelness per HUD Code?

**Answer**: 1/4 inch in 10 feet



**Question 2** (20 points):
Perform complete inspection using checklist


**Rubric**: Student correctly: uses measuring tools, identifies all defects, completes documentation accurately, determines pass/fail correctly

**Level**: Advanced

#### Learning Objectives

- Execute complete phase production process

**Materials**:

**Instructions**:

**2. Step-by-Step Process Walkthrough** (DEMONSTRATION) - 2 hours

**Materials**:
- Actual materials
- Tools
- Work instructions

**Instructions**:
Instructor performs complete process while explaining each step.




**3. Supervised Production** (HANDS-ON) - 4 hours

**Materials**:
- Full material kit
- All tools
- Work instructions
- Inspection forms

**Instructions**:
Student performs complete phase under supervision with coaching.


**âš ï¸ Safety Notes**:
- Continuous supervision required
- Stop immediately if quality issues arise
- Document all work performed



**4. Final Competency Demonstration** (PRACTICAL) - 1 hour

**Materials**:
- Complete material kit
- All tools
- Blank documentation

**Instructions**:
Student performs complete phase independently while being evaluated.




#### Assessment

**Type**: practical
**Passing Score**: 90%


**Question 1** (50 points):
Complete phase production process independently


**Rubric**: Evaluation criteria:
- Safety: 20 points (proper PPE, safe work practices, no violations)
- Quality: 20 points (meets all specifications, passes inspection)
- Process: 5 points (follows work instructions correctly)
- Documentation: 5 points (complete and accurate records)
- Efficiency: 5 points bonus (completes within target time)

## ðŸ† CERTIFICATION PROGRAM

### Phase 5 Production Technician

**Validity Period**: 12 months

### Certification Levels


#### Trainee

**Requirements**:
- Complete TM-01 Safety module
- Pass safety assessment

**Privileges**:
- Work under direct supervision
- Assist experienced workers


#### Technician I

**Requirements**:
- Complete all training modules
- Pass all assessments
- Complete 40 hours supervised work

**Privileges**:
- Work under general supervision
- Perform routine tasks
- Self-inspection


#### Technician II

**Requirements**:
- 6 months experience as Technician I
- Quality rating > 95%
- Complete advanced training

**Privileges**:
- Work independently
- Train others
- Perform complex tasks


#### Lead Technician

**Requirements**:
- 12 months as Technician II
- Leadership training
- Quality rating > 98%

**Privileges**:
- Supervise others
- Approve work
- Conduct training


### Recertification Requirements

- Complete annual refresher training (4 hours)
- Maintain quality performance above 95%
- No safety violations in past 12 months
- Complete any updated training modules

### Assessment Schedule

- **Initial**: Upon completion of all modules
- **Ongoing**: Quarterly performance reviews
- **Recertification**: Annual competency verification
