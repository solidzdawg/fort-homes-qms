## 1. Purpose
To ensure that all personnel performing work affecting product quality are competent based on appropriate education, training, skills, and experience.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all Fort Homes employees, contractors, and temporary workers involved in:
* Production operations
* Quality assurance/inspection
* Engineering and design review
* Material handling and storage
* Equipment operation and maintenance

## 3. Definitions
* **Competence**: Demonstrated ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results
* **On-the-Job Training (OJT)**: Practical skills training conducted at workstation by qualified trainer
* **Qualification**: Formal verification that an individual meets defined competency requirements
* **Training Matrix**: Document tracking required and completed training for each position/individual

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 COO (Chief Operating Officer)
* Establishes competency requirements for all positions
* Approves training plans and budgets
* Reviews training effectiveness quarterly
* Authorizes external training expenditures

### 4.2 QA Manager
* Maintains Training Matrix and training records
* Verifies inspector qualifications
* Coordinates regulatory training (DOH, OSHA, EPA)
* Conducts training effectiveness evaluations
* Ensures TPIA inspector credentials are current

### 4.3 Bay Supervisors
* Identify training needs for crew members
* Conduct or coordinate OJT for new hires
* Verify competency before releasing workers to unsupervised tasks
* Document skill verifications on Training Matrix

### 4.4 Individual Employees
* Participate actively in training programs
* Maintain personal certifications (e.g., forklift, electrical license)
* Request additional training when encountering unfamiliar tasks
* Comply with QMS requirements learned during training

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Competency Requirements

#### 5.1.1 Position-Specific Requirements

**Bay Supervisor:**
* Minimum 3 years residential construction experience
* Ability to read engineered plans and specifications
* Knowledge of IRC, NEC, IPC, IECC (relevant chapters)
* QMS Document Control training (SOP-001)
* NCR Process training (SOP-007)

**Production Crew Lead:**
* Minimum 1 year experience in assigned trade
* Basic blueprint reading
* Trade-specific code knowledge (NEC for electricians, IPC for plumbers)
* Tool safety and PPE usage
* Phase-specific Work Instructions (WIs)

**QA Manager (Zach Lamont):**
* ICC Residential Building Inspector certification (or equivalent)
* Minimum 5 years quality/inspection experience in manufactured housing
* Knowledge of DOH regulations (8 CCR 1302-14)
* Internal auditor training
* Root cause analysis methods

**QA Inspector (if hired):**
* Minimum 2 years construction inspection experience
* Blueprint reading proficiency
* Measurement and testing equipment calibration
* DOH inspection procedures

#### 5.1.2 General Requirements (All Employees)
* New Hire Orientation (safety, quality policy, ethics)
* Document Control (SOP-001) awareness
* Nonconformance identification and reporting (SOP-007)
* Emergency procedures and evacuation routes
* Hazard Communication (OSHA GHS)

### 5.2 Training Process

#### 5.2.1 New Hire Training
**Week 1:**
* Day 1: HR orientation, safety video series, facility tour
* Day 1: QMS overview presentation by QA Manager (30 minutes)
* Day 2-5: Shadow qualified worker in assigned bay/trade
* Day 5: Safety quiz (minimum 80% to proceed)

**Week 2-4:**
* Supervised OJT on assigned phase tasks
* Review relevant Work Instructions with Bay Supervisor
* Practice using measurement tools, inspection checklists
* Observe and assist with Hold Point inspections

**End of Month 1:**
* Competency evaluation by Bay Supervisor (practical skills test)
* Written exam on phase-specific quality standards
* If passed: authorized for independent work in that phase
* If failed: additional OJT week and retest

#### 5.2.2 Phase-Specific Training
Each production phase requires specific training before assignment:

**Phase 1 (Chassis & Floor):**
* WI-001: Floor Joist Assembly
* WI-002: Subfloor Installation
* ITP-CS-01: Floor System Inspection Criteria
* Adhesive application techniques
* Squaring and diagonal measurement methods

**Phase 2 (Wall Framing):**
* WI-003: Wall Panel Framing
* WI-004: Sheathing and WRB Installation
* ITP-CS-02 and ITP-CS-03: Wall Framing Inspection
* Stud layout and window/door rough opening sizing
* Hurricane tie and structural connector installation

**Phase 3 (Roof Framing):**
* WI-005: Truss or Rafter Installation
* WI-006: Roof Sheathing and Underlayment
* ITP-CS-04: Roof Framing Inspection
* Bearing connection details
* Attic ventilation calculation

**Phase 4 (MEP Rough-In):**
* Trade-specific licensing requirements:
  * Electricians: Colorado Journeyman or Master Electrician license
  * Plumbers: Colorado Journeyman or Master Plumber license
  * HVAC: EPA Section 608 certification (Type II minimum)
* NEC 2023 update training (if code cycle changed)
* Pressure testing procedures (water and DWV)
* ITP-E-01, ITP-E-02, ITP-P-01 through ITP-P-04, ITP-M-01 through ITP-M-03

**Phase 5 (Insulation):**
* RESNET Grade I installation standards
* Air sealing techniques and materials
* Vapor retarder requirements (climate zone specific)
* ITP-A-01, ITP-A-02

**Phase 6 (Drywall):**
* Fastener schedule and spacing
* Fire-rated assembly identification
* Level 4 vs. Level 5 finish standards
* ITP-A-03

**Phase 7 (Interior Finish):**
* Cabinet installation and leveling
* Flooring installation methods
* Paint quality standards
* GFCI/AFCI device testing
* ITP-A-04, ITP-A-05, ITP-E-03, ITP-P-05

**Phase 8 (Final Inspection):**
* Final blower door testing (RESNET standards)
* TPIA coordination and documentation
* DOH insignia application (QA Manager only)
* Module documentation package assembly
* ITP-A-06, ITP-CS-05

#### 5.2.3 Refresher Training
* Annual QMS refresher for all employees (4 hours, conducted in January)
* Code update training when IRC, NEC, IPC, or IECC is revised
* NCR review training after any significant or recurring nonconformance
* Tool and equipment training when new machinery is introduced

#### 5.2.4 External Training
* Trade license continuing education (as required by Colorado DORA)
* ICC certification renewals (every 3 years)
* Industry conferences (MHBA, ICC Annual Conference)
* Specialized topics (e.g., advanced air sealing, new HVAC technologies)

### 5.3 Training Verification and Records

#### 5.3.1 Training Matrix
QA Manager maintains master Training Matrix in spreadsheet format with columns:
* Employee Name
* Position/Title
* Hire Date
* Required Training Items (list per position)
* Completion Date
* Trainer/Evaluator Name
* Next Renewal Date (if applicable)
* Certification Numbers (licenses, ICC certs)

#### 5.3.2 Training Records File
For each training event, maintain:
* Attendance roster (sign-in sheet)
* Training agenda and materials
* Test/evaluation results (if applicable)
* Certificate of completion (for external training)
* Trainer qualifications

Retention: 3 years after employee separation

#### 5.3.3 Competency Evaluation
* Practical skills test: Observed performance of critical tasks (e.g., "Frame a window rough opening per plan")
* Written exam: 10-20 questions on quality standards, code requirements, and safety
* Pass criteria: 80% minimum score
* Retest: Allowed after additional training (1-2 weeks)

### 5.4 Training for Specific Roles

#### 5.4.1 QA Manager Qualification
* ICC Residential Building Inspector certification (or equivalent state-recognized credential)
* Internal Auditor training (8-hour course)
* DOH-specific training (attend CDOLA workshops or webinar series)
* Root cause analysis and CAPA methods
* Statistical process control basics

#### 5.4.2 Bay Supervisor Qualification
* Promoted from within after demonstrating competency in at least 4 of 8 phases
* Leadership and conflict resolution training
* Schedule management and critical path concepts
* Blueprint reading advanced course
* Authority to issue Red Tags for nonconformances

#### 5.4.3 TPIA Inspector Credentials
Fort Homes verifies that TPIA inspectors hold:
* ICC Residential Building Inspector certification, OR
* ICC Residential Plumbing Inspector + Residential Electrical Inspector, OR
* State of Colorado Professional Engineer (PE) license, OR
* State-approved equivalent credential under DOH regulations
* Proof of insurance and scope of authority letter from TPIA agency

Verification: QA Manager requests and files credentials before first inspection

### 5.5 Training Effectiveness Evaluation

#### 5.5.1 Metrics
* Training completion rate: % of employees current on required training (Target: 100%)
* Time to competency: Days from hire to qualification (Target: <30 days)
* NCR rate by employee: Defects caused per worker (Track high outliers for retraining)
* Rework rate: % of work requiring correction after inspection (Target: <5%)

#### 5.5.2 Quarterly Review
QA Manager and COO review:
* Training Matrix completion status
* Any employees behind on renewals
* NCR trends related to training gaps
* Feedback from Bay Supervisors on crew competence
* Adjustments to training programs based on data

#### 5.5.3 Continuous Improvement
* Annual survey of employees for training needs and suggestions
* Incident investigations include review of training adequacy
* CAPAs may require development of new Work Instructions or additional training modules

## 6. Temporary Workers and Contractors

### 6.1 Competency Verification
* Contractors must provide proof of trade licenses and insurance
* Temporary workers assigned only to tasks matching their demonstrated skills
* Contractor/temp work subject to same inspection and NCR process
* No temp worker assigned to a Hold Point inspection role

### 6.2 Orientation
* All contractors/temps receive abbreviated QMS orientation (30 minutes)
* Covers: safety, quality policy, NCR process, document access
* Sign acknowledgment form (retained by HR)

## 7. Records
* Training Matrix (master document, updated continuously)
* Individual training files (attendance, tests, certificates)
* Competency evaluation forms
* License and certification copies
* TPIA inspector credentials
* External training receipts and invoices
* Quarterly training effectiveness reports
